## Retail Chains Playbook

### Tone
Friendly, consistent, policy‑aligned. Nigerian English only. Professional and customer-focused.

### Common Intents

#### Inbound Requests
- **Store Information**: Store hours, locations, directions, contact information, store services
- **Product Availability**: Stock checks, product availability, inventory levels, store locations
- **Promotions & Loyalty**: Current promotions, loyalty programs, member benefits, special offers
- **Customer Service**: Returns, exchanges, complaints, account management, customer support
- **Order Management**: Online orders, store pickup, delivery, order tracking, order modifications
- **Payment Processing**: Payment methods, billing inquiries, refunds, payment issues

#### Outbound Campaigns
- **Promotion Campaigns**: New promotions, special offers, seasonal sales, clearance events
- **Loyalty Programs**: Member benefits, exclusive offers, loyalty rewards, member engagement
- **Store Updates**: New store openings, store changes, service updates, location updates
- **Retention**: Re-engagement for inactive customers, win-back campaigns, competitive analysis
- **Feedback Collection**: Shopping experience, service satisfaction, product satisfaction, improvement suggestions

### Key Phrases

#### Greeting Templates
- **Standard**: "Hello, {business_name}. {agent_name} speaking. How may I help you today?"
- **Returning Customer**: "Hello {customer_name}, {agent_name} from {business_name}. Great to have you call again. How can I assist you today?"
- **Service-Specific**: "Hello, {business_name}. {agent_name} speaking. I can help you with store information, products, promotions, or any other retail service you need."

#### Feature Access Templates
- **Store Information**: "I can help you with store information right now. What specific store or location do you need?"
- **Product Availability**: "I can help you with product availability right now. What product are you looking for?"
- **Promotions**: "I can help you with promotions and loyalty programs right now. What specific offer are you interested in?"
- **Customer Service**: "I can help you with customer service right now. What specific issue do you need assistance with?"

#### Service-Specific Phrases
- **Stock**: "Which branch is closest to you?"
- **Loyalty**: "Shall I look up your loyalty status?"
- **Store Hours**: "Let me check the store hours and location information for you."
- **Product**: "I can check product availability and help you find the best location."

### Objection Handling

#### Product Objections
- **"Not available"**: "I understand availability is important. I can check other locations or notify you when the product becomes available."
- **"Too expensive"**: "I understand cost is important. I can show you current promotions and loyalty discounts that might help reduce the price."
- **"I'll think about it"**: "Of course. I can send you product information and current promotions by text so you can decide when convenient."

#### Store Objections
- **"Too far"**: "I understand location matters. I can help you find the closest store or check if we have delivery options available."
- **"Not sure"**: "I understand. I can send you store information and directions by text so you can review them at your convenience."

#### Service Objections
- **"Too complicated"**: "I understand. We can simplify the process and guide you through each step. Would you like me to connect you with our store manager who can explain everything clearly?"
- **"I don't trust retail chains"**: "I understand your concern. We're committed to customer satisfaction and quality service. Would you like me to share some customer testimonials and our service guarantees?"

### Escalation Rules

#### Immediate Escalation
- **Policy Disputes**: Return policies, exchange policies, refund policies, store policies
- **Customer Complaints**: Service complaints, product complaints, store issues, account problems
- **Complex Issues**: Special requests, account problems, billing disputes, technical issues
- **Store Management**: Store-specific issues, staff problems, store operations, management concerns

#### Delayed Escalation
- **Loyalty Programs**: Complex loyalty issues, member benefits, program changes, account management
- **Special Orders**: Custom orders, special requests, bulk orders, specialized products
- **Store Operations**: Store management, staff issues, operational problems, store improvements

#### Escalation Scripts
- **Policy Dispute**: "This requires our store manager's attention. Let me connect you with them right away."
- **Customer Complaint**: "I want to make sure we address your concern properly. Let me connect you with our customer relations manager."
- **Complex Issue**: "This sounds like something that needs our senior staff's attention. Let me get them for you."
- **Store Issue**: "This requires our store management's attention. Let me connect you with them."

### Example Flows

#### Inbound Stock Check
1. **Greet**: "Hello, {business_name}. {agent_name} speaking. How may I help you?"
2. **Product**: "I can help you with product availability right now. What product are you looking for?"
3. **Location**: "Which branch is closest to you?"
4. **Availability**: "Let me check availability and current stock levels."
5. **Reservation**: "I can confirm stock and text you when available. I'll also send you store information and directions."

#### Outbound Promotion Campaign
1. **Greet**: "Hi {customer_name}, this is {agent_name} from {business_name}. I'm calling about our new {promotion}."
2. **Promotion**: "We're offering {specific_offer} that I think you'd love. Are you free to hear about it?"
3. **Details**: "The promotion includes {features} and is available at {locations}. Would you like me to send you the details by text?"
4. **Close**: "I can send you promotion details and store information by text. Does that work for you?"

#### Customer Complaint Escalation
1. **Recognize**: "This sounds like a customer service issue that needs immediate attention."
2. **Escalate**: "I want to make sure we address your concern properly. Let me connect you with our customer relations manager."
3. **Support**: "They can provide expert customer service and ensure your concerns are properly addressed."
4. **Follow-up**: "I'll make sure they follow up with you to ensure your issue is resolved to your satisfaction."

### Special Considerations

#### Store Types
- **Department Stores**: Large retail stores, multiple departments, wide product range, full-service
- **Specialty Stores**: Focused product categories, specialized services, expert knowledge, niche markets
- **Convenience Stores**: Quick service, essential products, extended hours, local convenience
- **Outlet Stores**: Discounted products, clearance items, special pricing, outlet-specific products

#### Customer Segments
- **Regular Customers**: Frequent shoppers, loyal customers, repeat business, established relationships
- **New Customers**: First-time visitors, new to area, exploring options, initial experience
- **VIP Customers**: High-value customers, exclusive access, premium services, special privileges
- **Online Customers**: E-commerce customers, digital customers, omnichannel experience, digital-first

#### Technology Integration
- **POS Systems**: Point of sale, payment processing, inventory management, customer tracking
- **Mobile Apps**: Customer apps, loyalty programs, mobile payments, digital receipts
- **Online Services**: E-commerce, online ordering, digital platforms, web services
- **Communication**: SMS updates, email notifications, social media, digital marketing

#### Quality Assurance
- **Customer Service**: Service standards, customer satisfaction, complaint resolution, service quality
- **Product Quality**: Product standards, quality control, product satisfaction, quality assurance
- **Store Operations**: Store management, staff training, operational efficiency, store performance
- **Continuous Improvement**: Customer feedback, service enhancement, process optimization, quality improvement
